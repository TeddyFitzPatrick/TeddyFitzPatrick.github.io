Here are my course recommendations if you're interested: Out of the courses that I was not required to take, I would highly recommend both Modern Physics I and Gen & Analytical Chem I. Both of which are challenging, but if you are the type of person who likes science, these classes are really enjoyable. In Modern Physics I, we delved into many scientific concepts that I have heard about, but never got to explore in depth (i.e. Schrodinger's Equation, Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle, Bohr Model, Relativity). Gen & Analytical Chem I was difficult considering it was primarily for chem/bio majors, but it was fun learning about topics like combustion, enthalpy/entropy, and stoichiometry.
Out of my liberal arts courses, my favorite is definitely Public History & Public Debate with Professor Michael Brown. Instead of busywork and essays, the course consisted of class discussion which made it more engaging. We covered a lot of local Rochester history which was interesting.
Out of my CS courses, my favorite is Computer Science for AP Students. For a first-year class, we covered a lot of ground, with graph traversals, heaps, networking, multi-threading, GitHub, and backtracking. Not an overly easy or difficult class with fair grading.
