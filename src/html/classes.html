Here are my course recommendations if you're interested: Science: I would highly recommend both Modern Physics I and Gen & Analytical Chem I. Both of which are challenging, but if you are the type of person who likes science, these classes are really enjoyable. In Modern Physics I, we explored a variety scientific concepts that I was familiar with, but never got to explore in depth (i.e. Schrodinger's Equation, Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle, Bohr Model, Relativity). Gen & Analytical Chem I was difficult, I believe it was primarily for weeding out chem/bio majors. Nevertheless, everything we covered was fascinating; to name a few: combustion, enthalpy/entropy, and stoichiometry.
Liberal Arts: I've taken a handful, maybe 10, liberal-arts courses at RIT. I would recommend Introduction to Film and Public History & Public Debate with Professor Michael Brown. Great Professor and we got to learn about local Rochester history which was fun.
